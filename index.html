Know Your Doctor

A Legacy of Precision & Compassionate Care

Dr. Rajendra Prasad Bhupathi is a Senior Consultant and Head of the Department of Surgical Gastroenterology, Minimal Access & Robotic Surgery at CARE Hospitals, Malakpet, Hyderabad. With over 20 years of total experience — including 8 years of teaching — he has performed more than 5,000 major and complex surgeries with an outstanding success rate of over 99%.

His special interests include Minimal Access Surgery, HPB (Liver, Gallbladder, Pancreas) & Colorectal (Large Intestine, Rectum, Anal canal) Surgery. He has trained extensively in Laparoscopic/Minimal Access surgery, GI & HPB Oncology, Colorectal Surgery & Colorectal Oncology at excellent centres, and regularly presents papers at national and international conferences.

Areas of Excellence

Surgical Specialities

Comprehensive expertise across the full spectrum of gastrointestinal, hepato-biliary, and laparoscopic surgical care.

🔬

Laparoscopic & Robotic Surgery

Advanced keyhole and robotic surgical techniques for minimal scarring, reduced pain, faster discharge, and superior outcomes across a wide range of GI conditions.

🎗️

GI & HPB Oncosurgery

Curative and palliative surgical management of cancers of the stomach, colon, rectum, liver, pancreas, and biliary tract with a multidisciplinary team approach.

🫁

Liver, Pancreas & Biliary Surgery

Complex HPB surgeries including liver resections, Whipple's procedure, distal pancreatectomy, biliary reconstructions, and management of portal hypertension.

🩺

Colorectal Surgery

Expert management of colorectal cancer, IBD, diverticulitis, and anorectal conditions including piles, fistulas, fissures, and rectal prolapse.

🔗

Hernia Surgery

Minimally invasive mesh repair for all types of hernias — inguinal, femoral, umbilical, incisional, ventral — including complex recurrent hernias.

💊

Upper GI Surgery

Surgical management of GERD, achalasia, oesophageal and gastric conditions including anti-reflux procedures, Heller's myotomy, and bariatric surgery.
